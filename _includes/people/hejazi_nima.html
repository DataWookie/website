Nima Hejazi (https://nimahejazi.org) is a PhD student in Biostatistics at the UC Berkeley School of Public Health (http://sph.berkeley.edu/). An applied statistician with diverse interests, he aims to build tools and develop theory at the intersection of nonparametrics, machine learning, and causal inference. Nima enjoys programming in R, Python, C++, and, recently, Julia, and he is an advocate for openness and reproducibility in data-centric sciences.
